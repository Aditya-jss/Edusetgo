United States of America

The United States offers unparalleled academic excellence, hosting world-renowned universities and fostering innovation across diverse disciplines.

4,000+ Institutions
1M+ International Students
$20K-$60K Annual Tuition
1

Why Study in the United States?

Renowned for its academic prestige, the U.S. offers:

  • Top-tier universities (e.g., Harvard, MIT, Stanford)
  • Flexible curricula with diverse academic programs
  • Extensive research and innovation opportunities
  • Robust career and networking prospects
2

Exams Required

Standardized tests for admission include:

TOEFL (80+) IELTS (6.5+) GRE (300+) GMAT (600+) Duolingo (105+)
3

Admission Process

Follow these steps to apply:

  • Research universities and programs
  • Complete required standardized tests
  • Prepare application materials (SOP, LORs, transcripts)
  • Apply via university portals or Common App
  • Secure an F-1 student visa
4

Top Cities

Key destinations for students include:

  • Boston: Academic hub with MIT and Harvard
  • New York: Global business and cultural center
  • San Francisco: Technology and innovation hub
  • Los Angeles: Arts and entertainment capital
5

Top Jobs

High-demand career paths include:

  • Software Engineer
  • Data Scientist
  • Business Analyst
  • Medical Professional
6

Average Salaries

Graduate salary ranges:

  • Software Engineer: $90K-$150K/year
  • Data Scientist: $85K-$130K/year
  • Business Analyst: $70K-$110K/year
  • Medical Professional: $100K-$250K/year
7

Application to Departure Timeline

Typical timeline:

  • 12-18 months prior: Research and exam preparation
  • 9-12 months prior: Complete exams, select universities
  • 6-9 months prior: Submit applications
  • 3-6 months prior: Receive admission, apply for visa
  • 1-2 months prior: Arrange travel and accommodation
